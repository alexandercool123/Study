CROSS-LECTURE NOTES:
- Q8b is the big one: ROLES (resource owner, client, authorization server, resource server), GRANTS (authorization code; note implicit & ROPC are deprecated — say so), client profiles (public vs confidential), TOKENS (access token, refresh token; bearer), ENDPOINTS (authorization, token), SCOPES, information flow.
- Outdated grants: "implicit" and "resource owner password credential" are no longer recommended — mention but don't dwell.
- PKCE (RFC 7636) protects public clients against auth-code interception; DPoP (RFC 9449) binds tokens to a key (sender-constrained) vs bearer tokens.
- OAuth is authorization/delegation, NOT authentication — that gap is why OpenID Connect exists (lecture 11, Q9). Note this.
